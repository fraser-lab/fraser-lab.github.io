description: "Taylor graduated from UCLA with a degree in Biophysics. During his undergraduate training, he worked on computational models of the preBötzinger Complex neural circuit under the mentorship of the late [Dr. Alexander Levine](http://alevine.chem.ucla.edu/). Afterwards, Taylor pursued a masters in Theoretical Physics at Utrecht University in the Netherlands, where he completed his thesis on pattern formation in growing baceterial colonies under the mentorship of [Dr. Joost de Graaf](https://webspace.science.uu.nl/~graaf156/group.html) and [Dr. Tyler Shendruk](https://tnshendruk.com/) (University of Edinburgh). Following his masters, Taylor completed a reseach internship at the Vienna Doctoral School of Physics on the sympathetic cooling of nanobioparticles under the mentorship of [Dr. Markus Arndt](https://www.quantumnano.at/) and [Dr. Benjamin Stickler](https://www.uni-ulm.de/en/nawi/institute-for-complex-quantum-systems/team/members-of-the-institute/prof-dr-benjamin-stickler/) (Ulm University)\


Now, in the Fraser Lab, Taylor aims to study the protein conformational dynamics that occur inside of protein crystals using molecular dynamics simulations and the theory of X-Ray diffraction.

Outside of lab, Taylor enjoys hiking, trail running, watching movies, reading, and playing guitar. He is also quite fond of happy hour."

description: "Karen earned her B.S. in Biochemistry from the University of Texas at El Paso, where she was first introduced to structural biology through studies of molecular chaperones. She completed her Ph.D. in Biochemistry and Molecular Biophysics at Caltech under the mentorship of [Prof. Bil Clemons](https://clemonslab.caltech.edu/). Her doctoral research focused on studying membrane proteins essential for peptidoglycan biosynthesis and their interactions with phage-derived “protein antibiotics” by cryoEM.

She subsequently worked with [Dr. Gabriel Lander](https://www.lander-lab.com/), applying cryo-EM to study CYP3A4 and other proteins involved in cellular maintenance. In the Fraser Lab, Karen continues her research on the cytochrome P450 protein family as part of the AVOID-OME project.

In her spare time, she enjoys watching movies, exploring new craft hobbies, gardening, and spending time with friends."

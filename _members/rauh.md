description: "Arriën graduated from the University of Groningen with a BSc degree in molecular biology. He worked on validation of MD simulations of a molecular switch molecule by predicting experimental observations, and integration of cryo-EM and molecular simulations in the [Marrink Lab](https://www.rug.nl/research/molecular-dynamics/). Subsequently, he obtained an MSc degree in bioinformatics from the VU Amsterdam & the University of Amsterdam. For his master thesis research he joined the [Zacharias lab](https://www.bio.nat.tum.de/t38/home/) at the Technical University Munich where he studied drug binding to gamma-secretase using MD simulations and docking.


For his PhD research, Arriën joined the [Lindorff-Larsen lab](https://www1.bio.ku.dk/english/research/bms/sbinlab/kll/) where he studied the relationship between the sequence, dynamics and function (or often phase separation behaviour) of disordered proteins (eg. hnRNPA1, alpha-synuclein, TDP-43, etc.) applying coarse-grained simulation approaches informed by experimental data. In his work he has a special interest in how 'environmental factors', such as macromolecular crowding and post-translational modification, modify protein dynamics.


In the Fraser lab, Arriën will join the efforts on the [diffUSE project](https://diffuse.science), pushing our abilities to learn protein dynamics from crystallograpgy data with molecular simulations and AI. Outside of work, he enjoys playing the cello (preferably in an orchestra), going to concerts, biking, and if there's an opportunity going out in nature, skiing or sailing."

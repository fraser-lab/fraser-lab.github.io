description: "Art is a well-traveled crystallographer and structural biologist with experience ranging from cloning to coding. While pursuing his Bachelor's degree in Chemistry at UC Davis, he received an HHMI summer fellowship to work on a protein structure prediction project. In the process, he came across [Tom Alber's work on protein stability](https://pubs.acs.org/doi/pdf/10.1021/bi00387a002) and was forever hooked on structural biology as a result. He obtained his PhD with [Alice Vrielink](https://research-repository.uwa.edu.au/en/persons/alice-vrielink/) at UC Santa Cruz, working on sub-Ångstrom-resolution structures of flavoproteins. He then studied DNA-remodeling proteins as an American Cancer Society post-doctoral scholar with [James Berger](https://berger.med.jhmi.edu/) at UC Berkeley, worked on serial crystallography methods with [Axel Brunger](https://atbweb.stanford.edu/) at Stanford Medical School, and developed automated data processing and analysis pipelines in [Aina Cohen's Structural and Molecular Biology group](smb.slac.stanford.edu) at the Stanford Synchrotron Radiation Lightsource. 

Art joins the Fraser Lab in a project scientist role, bringing his varied and extensive experience in structural biology to the fragment screening and diffuse scattering projects."
